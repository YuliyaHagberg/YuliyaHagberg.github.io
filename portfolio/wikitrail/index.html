UX / UI Design

Wikitrail

A mobile application enhancing hiking and outdoor experiences along Solanderleden, a trail network in Norrbotten, Sweden. The app combines a hiking guide with social media functionality, allowing users to navigate the trail, share experiences, and interact in real-time.

About this project

Wikitrail was developed to improve and expand the range of hiking activities along Solanderleden, connecting trails, parks, villages, and local businesses into a single digital experience.

The project aimed to:

  • Enhance trail experiences for hikers and activity participants.
  • Facilitate community interaction through a social-media-like platform.
  • Enable personalized experiences and data-driven insights for users.

Using agile project management, our team organized the development into iterative sprints. I contributed to:

  • Translating user needs and stakeholder requirements into detailed user stories and functional features.
  • Designing and prototyping the mobile app in Figma, including the interactive map, user profiles, posting flow, and chat functionality.
  • Conducting user testing to refine usability and visual design based on feedback.
  • Integrating visual inspiration from popular social media apps to enhance recognition and ease of use while maintaining a nature-oriented aesthetic.

Key functionalities include:

  • Interactive map with geolocation for hikers.
  • Personalized profiles and experience tracking.
  • Photo and video sharing of trail experiences.
  • Real-time chat and user interaction.
  • Tailored notifications and recommendations based on user preferences.

The project successfully created a tool that transforms users from passive observers into active participants, fostering community and enhancing engagement with the trail network.

Design choices

The design of Wikitrail was guided by the goal of creating an intuitive and engaging experience that feels both familiar and closely connected to nature. The information architecture was structured to clearly separate key areas such as map navigation, user profiles, social content, and messaging, ensuring that users can quickly access essential features—especially in outdoor contexts where ease of use is critical. Visually, the interface draws inspiration from natural elements, incorporating earthy tones, organic shapes, and typography that subtly reflects the outdoor environment, helping to reinforce the app’s purpose and atmosphere.

To reduce the learning curve, interaction patterns were inspired by widely used social media platforms, allowing users to easily understand how to post content, interact with others, and navigate the app without prior instruction. At the same time, these familiar patterns were adapted to support exploration and movement, encouraging users to actively engage with their surroundings rather than passively consume content. Accessibility was also considered in the design process, with clear visual hierarchies and interface cues to support usability, while acknowledging areas for future improvement such as enhanced support for assistive technologies. Overall, the design choices were made to balance usability, engagement, and a strong sense of place, resulting in an experience that supports both exploration and community-building.
