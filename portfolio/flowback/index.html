Flowback — Digital Democracy Platform

An open-source platform for better organisational decision-making. Lead frontend developer and sole UI/UX designer — responsible for the full interface design in Figma, implementing it in TypeScript/SvelteKit/React, and integrating backend APIs.

About this project

Flowback is an open-source decision system that aims to transform collective decision making through the innovation Predictive Liquid Democracy (PLD). Our platform combines fast and flexible decentralised decision making through liquid democracy with prediction markets that crowdsource the wisdom of the masses and artificial agents.

By using PLD, Flowback aims to create decision-making processes that are resistant to misinformation, aligned with collective goals, and informed by ideas and quality predictions from not only human agents but also virtual artificial agents. The goal is to turn organisations into Bayesian updaters that make the optimal decision with regards to the available information and with regards to the organisation’s goals as expressed by the members in a democratic voting process.

Flowback serves a diverse audience, including non-profits, decentralized networks, political parties, educational institutions, corporations, and government entities, all looking to enhance their governance structures and tap into collective intelligence. The system aims to be scalable and adaptable to various sectors, making it a versatile tool for entities of all sizes.

Two versions of the interface
One-group version / Single organisation

The single-organisation version of Flowback is designed for use within one organisation, such as a non-profit, company, or association. It focuses on enabling structured internal decision-making through proposals, discussions, and voting. The interface emphasizes clarity and simplicity, guiding users step by step through the decision-making process while reducing cognitive load through clear hierarchy and progressive disclosure.

Several-groups version / Multi-organisation

The multi-organisation version extends Flowback into a more complex system where multiple groups can coexist and interact. Users can navigate between organisations and participate in different decision spaces, requiring a more advanced information architecture. The design introduces clear structural layers, navigation patterns, and visual cues to maintain usability while supporting scalability and coordination across organisations.

Design choices

The design of Flowback is centered around transforming complex and abstract decision-making processes into structured, intuitive user experiences. A key priority was reducing cognitive load while still supporting advanced concepts such as liquid democracy, delegation, and prediction-based inputs.

Information architecture was designed to reflect the natural flow of decision-making: from proposal creation, to discussion, to voting and results. Content is grouped into clear sections and presented progressively, allowing users to focus on one step at a time rather than being overwhelmed by the full process.

Visual design and hierarchy play an important role in guiding users. Consistent layouts, spacing, and typography create a predictable interface, while emphasis is used to highlight key actions and important information. The goal was to make complex systems feel calm, structured, and approachable.

Interaction patterns were designed to guide users through the system. Step-by-step flows, clear call-to-action elements, and feedback states help users understand both what they can do and what is happening in the system. Familiar UI patterns were combined with custom components tailored to decision-making workflows.

Accessibility and usability were also key considerations. The design emphasizes readability, clear contrast, and consistent navigation, ensuring that users with different levels of technical experience can effectively participate. Special attention was given to making the system understandable even for users unfamiliar with concepts like delegation or predictive voting.

Overall, the design balances flexibility and structure, enabling powerful decision-making capabilities while maintaining a user-friendly and intuitive experience.
