HCI Research: Decision Support System Design

Bachelor's thesis investigating how the design of decision support systems can be optimised, using the Flowback platform as a case study. Conducted qualitative and quantitative user research, published via DiVA portal.

About this project

Flowback was developed as part of my thesis, focusing on the design of user-centered interfaces for group decision-support systems. The project addresses the challenge of facilitating structured collaboration in situations where multiple participants need to evaluate information, share perspectives, and reach decisions efficiently.

The goal was to design an interface that reduces cognitive load, improves clarity, and supports decision-making processes through well-structured interaction patterns. The project followed a user-centered design approach, where the system was iteratively developed and refined based on design principles, analysis of existing systems, and continuous evaluation.

My contributions included:

  • Designing the full mobile interface in Figma, including workflows for decision-making, input, and feedback.
  • Structuring complex information into clear, digestible views to support group understanding and alignment.
  • Developing interaction patterns that guide users through step-by-step decision processes.
  • Applying UX principles and research to balance functionality, usability, and clarity.

The resulting prototype demonstrates how digital tools can support collaborative decision-making by transforming abstract processes into structured, user-friendly interactions.

Design choices

The design of Flowback was driven by the need to simplify complex group decision-making processes and present them in a structured, intuitive way. A key focus was on reducing cognitive load by breaking down decisions into smaller, manageable steps, allowing users to focus on one part of the process at a time rather than being overwhelmed by information. The interface emphasizes clarity through strong visual hierarchy, consistent layouts, and clearly defined interaction flows that guide users through each stage of collaboration.

To support usability in group contexts, the design prioritizes transparency and shared understanding, ensuring that users can easily interpret information, compare alternatives, and follow how decisions evolve over time. Familiar interaction patterns were combined with structured workflows to create a balance between ease of use and functional depth. Visual consistency and spacing were used to create a calm and focused experience, while interactive elements were designed to clearly communicate available actions and system states. Overall, the design choices aim to transform a potentially complex and abstract process into a guided, accessible, and efficient user experience.
